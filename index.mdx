Connect an RTL-SDR, HackRF, Airspy, or SDRPlay device and start decoding P25, DMR, MPT-1327, LTR, and other trunked radio protocols in real time — complete with audio playback, recording, and live streaming to Broadcastify or Icecast.

## What SDRTrunk does

SDRTrunk monitors trunked radio systems — the kind used by public safety agencies, utilities, and businesses. When a call goes out, the radio system dynamically assigns it to a free frequency (a "trunk"). SDRTrunk follows these calls across frequencies automatically, so you hear complete conversations rather than fragments.

## Get started in 3 steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and install">
    Grab the latest release for your operating system from the [releases page](https://github.com/DSheirer/sdrtrunk/releases). SDRTrunk bundles its own Java runtime — no separate installation needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your SDR hardware">
    Plug in your RTL-SDR, HackRF, Airspy, or SDRPlay device. SDRTrunk auto-detects supported hardware on startup. See the [hardware guide](/hardware/overview) for driver requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure a channel and start decoding">
    Open the Playlist Editor, create an alias list, add a channel with the correct frequency and protocol, and click **Enable**. The spectral display shows your signal and audio begins playing immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key features

- **Multi-protocol decoding** — P25 Phase 1 and 2, DMR, MPT-1327, LTR, LTR-Net, NBFM, MDC-1200, Fleetsync, and more
- **Polyphase channelizer** — decode dozens of channels simultaneously from one tuner
- **Real-time spectral display** — waterfall and spectrum view with zoom and pan
- **Alias management** — assign names, colors, and actions to talkgroups and radio IDs
- **Audio streaming** — broadcast live to Broadcastify, Icecast, OpenMHz, and Shoutcast
- **Cross-platform** — runs on Windows, Linux, and macOS (including Apple Silicon)
- **Headless mode** — run without a GUI for server or embedded deployments
