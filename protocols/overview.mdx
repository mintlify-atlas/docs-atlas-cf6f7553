SDRTrunk supports a wide range of analog and digital radio protocols, spanning trunked systems, conventional voice channels, and in-band signaling. This page summarizes every protocol available in SDRTrunk and links to dedicated pages for the protocols that need detailed setup instructions.

## Trunked protocols

Trunked systems dynamically assign calls to available frequencies using a control channel. SDRTrunk monitors the control channel and automatically follows calls to traffic channels as they are assigned.

| Protocol | Bit rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| P25 Phase 1 (APCO-25) | 9,600 bps | C4FM or simulcast (LSM) modulation |
| P25 Phase 2 (APCO-25 P2) | 12,000 bps | TDMA, 2 timeslots per 12.5 kHz channel |
| DMR | 9,600 bps | TDMA, 2 timeslots per 12.5 kHz channel |
| MPT-1327 | 1,200 bps | Analog trunking standard |
| LTR | 300 bps | Logic Trunked Radio, inband signaling |
| LTR-Net | 300 bps | Enhanced LTR with network messaging |
| Passport | 300 bps | Similar inband trunking approach to LTR |

<Note>
  To find the control channel frequency and system type for your local area, check [RadioReference.com](https://www.radioreference.com) and search for your county or city.
</Note>

## Digital voice protocols

SDRTrunk decodes digitally encoded voice for P25 and DMR. Both standards use Multi-Band Excitation (MBE) codecs — IMBE for P25 Phase 1, AMBE+ for P25 Phase 2, and AMBE+ for DMR.

<Warning>
  Digital voice audio playback requires the optional **JMBE library**. Without it, SDRTrunk decodes signaling and metadata but produces no audio for P25 or DMR calls. Download JMBE from the [SDRTrunk releases page](https://github.com/DSheirer/sdrtrunk/releases) and place it alongside the SDRTrunk JAR.
</Warning>

## Conventional and analog protocols

SDRTrunk can also tune conventional (non-trunked) channels:

| Protocol | Description |
|---|---|
| NBFM | Narrowband FM — standard analog voice |
| AM | Amplitude modulation — used in aviation and some legacy systems |

## Auxiliary signaling decoders

These decoders run alongside a primary decoder and extract in-band signaling from the demodulated audio channel:

| Protocol | Bit rate | Description |
|---|---|---|
| MDC-1200 | 1,200 bps | Motorola radio ID signaling |
| Fleetsync II | 1,200 bps | Kenwood radio ID and status signaling |
| DCS | 134 bps | Digital Coded Squelch tone identification |
| Tait 1200 | 1,200 bps | Tait radio ID signaling |
