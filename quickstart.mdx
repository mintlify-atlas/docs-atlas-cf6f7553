---
title: 'Quickstart: from SDR hardware to decoded audio in SDRTrunk'
description: 'Connect your SDR, find a local radio system, configure a channel in the Playlist Editor, and hear decoded audio — from launch to audio in under 10 minutes.'
---

This page walks you through everything you need to go from a freshly installed SDRTrunk to actively decoding audio on your first channel. You will launch the application, confirm your hardware is detected, find a signal in the spectral display, create a channel in the Playlist Editor, and start decoding.

<Note>
You need to know the frequency and protocol of a local radio system before you can configure a channel. [RadioReference.com](https://www.radioreference.com) is the most comprehensive public database of radio system frequencies in North America. Create a free account to search for systems near you.
</Note>

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch SDRTrunk and confirm your tuner is detected">
    Start SDRTrunk using the launcher for your operating system:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Windows">
        Open the extracted SDRTrunk folder, navigate to the `bin` directory, and double-click `sdrtrunk.bat`.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Linux">
        ```bash
        ~/sdrtrunk/bin/sdrtrunk
        ```
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="macOS">
        ```bash
        ~/sdrtrunk/bin/sdrtrunk
        ```
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    When the application opens, the title bar updates to show your tuner's name, for example **SDRTrunk - Generic RTL2832U**. If no tuner name appears, SDRTrunk did not detect your hardware.

    To see all detected tuners, open **View > Tuners** in the menu bar. Each connected and recognized SDR appears in the list. Selecting one makes it the active tuner for the spectral display.

    <Warning>
      If your tuner is not detected, confirm it is plugged in and that drivers are installed correctly. See the [installation guide](/installation) for per-OS driver instructions.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the spectral display and find a signal">
    The spectral display at the top of the main window shows a live FFT spectrum and waterfall for the active tuner. Signals appear as peaks in the spectrum and bright traces on the waterfall.

    To center the display on a known frequency:

    1. Right-click inside the spectral display.
    2. Select **Go to frequency** and enter your target frequency in MHz (for example, `460.100`).

    Trunked radio control channels and traffic channels appear as consistent spikes at specific frequencies. A P25 control channel typically shows a stable carrier that does not vary much over time.

    <Tip>
      You can click directly on any signal in the spectral display to inspect it. SDRTrunk will show decoded frames in the **Now Playing** panel as soon as a matching channel is configured.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the Playlist Editor">
    Open the Playlist Editor using **View > Playlist Editor** in the menu bar.

    The Playlist Editor is where you define all channel configurations, alias lists, and streaming destinations. Your configuration is saved in a playlist file (`.xml`) that SDRTrunk loads automatically on startup.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create an alias list">
    Alias lists let you assign names, colors, and icons to talkgroup IDs and radio IDs so decoded traffic appears with meaningful labels.

    In the Playlist Editor:

    1. Click the **Aliases** tab.
    2. Click **New Alias List** and give it a descriptive name such as `Local P25 System`.
    3. Optionally, add individual aliases by clicking **Add Alias**, entering a talkgroup ID or radio ID, and providing a name and color.

    You can add or edit aliases at any time, including while channels are actively decoding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a channel">
    In the Playlist Editor:

    1. Click the **Channels** tab.
    2. Click **New Channel**.
    3. Enter a descriptive name for the channel, such as `County P25 Control`.
    4. In the **Source** section, set the **Tuner** to your connected SDR and enter the **Frequency** in Hz (for example, `460100000` for 460.100 MHz).
    5. In the **Decoder** section, select the protocol that matches your radio system:
       - Use **APCO-25** for P25 Phase 1 systems
       - Use **APCO-25 Phase 2** for P25 Phase 2 systems
       - Use **DMR** for Digital Mobile Radio systems
       - Use **MPT-1327**, **LTR**, **LTR-Net**, or **Passport** for their respective trunked protocols
       - Use **NBFM** for standard narrowband FM analog voice
       - Use **AM** for amplitude modulation voice (such as aircraft)
    6. In the **Aliases** section, select the alias list you created in the previous step.
    7. Click **Save** to save the channel configuration.

    <Info>
      For trunked systems (P25, DMR, MPT-1327, LTR, LTR-Net), configure the channel using the **control channel frequency**. SDRTrunk will automatically follow traffic channels as the system assigns them.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable the channel and start decoding">
    In the **Channels** tab of the Playlist Editor, find your new channel in the list. Click the toggle or checkbox in the **Enabled** column to enable it.

    SDRTrunk immediately starts decoding the configured channel. The controller panel in the main window shows active decoding activity, and decoded messages begin appearing in the event log.

    To start decoding automatically every time SDRTrunk launches, right-click the channel and select **Auto-start**. SDRTrunk will present a countdown dialog at startup and then start the channel automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Listen to audio playback">
    Decoded audio plays through your system's default audio output device automatically. When a talkgroup becomes active on a trunked system, SDRTrunk routes the audio to your speakers or headphones in real time.

    <Tip>
      The **Now Playing** panel in the controller panel shows the currently active talkgroup, its alias name (if configured), and a timeline of recent calls. You can toggle the detail tabs in this panel using **View > Show Now Playing Channel Details**.
    </Tip>

    To review audio after it has played, open **View > Audio Recordings** to browse recorded files in your file manager. SDRTrunk saves recordings to the `SDRTrunk/recordings` directory in your home folder by default.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common first-run issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No audio is playing even though the channel shows decoding activity">
    Check that your system's default audio output device is not muted and that SDRTrunk's audio output is routed correctly. Open **View > User Preferences** and review the **Playback** settings to confirm the correct output device is selected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The channel shows no decoding activity at all">
    Confirm the frequency is correct and that the signal is visible in the spectral display. For trunked systems, verify you are pointed at the control channel rather than a traffic channel. Check that the tuner gain is not set too low — open the tuner configuration and increase gain if the noise floor appears very low.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="P25 audio sounds garbled or produces no voice">
    P25 Phase 2 and some P25 Phase 1 systems use IMBE or AMBE voice codecs that require the JMBE library. Open **View > User Preferences > JMBE Library** and follow the instructions to download and install the JMBE library. Restart SDRTrunk after installing it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SDRTrunk keeps losing the control channel">
    Your tuner's frequency reference may need PPM correction. Use a known signal to calibrate: open the tuner configuration from the **View > Tuners** menu, and adjust the **PPM** (parts per million) offset until the signal centers correctly in the spectral display.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
